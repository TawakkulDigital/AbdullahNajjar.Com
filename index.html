Faith & Education

Abdullah Najjar is the former Assistant Imam and Youth Director at the American Muslim Institute. He is currently a graduate student of Religious Studies at Chicago Theological Seminary. He previously completed 2 years of law school in Houston, focusing on civil and human rights. His Islamic education includes studies with institutions like Mishkah University, Al-Salam Institute, AlMaghrib, Qalam, Bayyinah, and Islamic Online University (Diploma).

Community Impact & Leadership

Abdullah has been actively serving the Muslim community for over 20 years — teaching and mentoring more than 1,000 students one-on-one. He has spoken at major universities including Harvard, Berkeley, Emory, Rice, and Vanderbilt, and at high schools and masjids across the U.S. He has helped raise hundreds of thousands for charities like Islamic Relief and Human Concern International, and has campaigned for Muslim candidates and lobbied over 50 politicians on issues like Palestine, prison conditions, and school bullying.

  • Learn More

Media, Marketing & Influence

Abdullah has worked in brokering, marketing, and sales for brands such as Layne’s, Black Seed Oil Gummies, Mubarak Mortgage, and Mecca Digital. He has built a social media influencer network with over 100 million combined followers. He has been featured by Harvard documentaries, PBS, NPR, NRG Radio, university publications, and local TV. He has spoken at major conventions like ICNA Houston and the Texas Dawah Convention.
